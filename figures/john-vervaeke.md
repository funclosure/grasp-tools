# John Vervaeke

## Why he matters here

John Vervaeke matters to Grasp Tools because he provides a richer account of knowing than modern information-centered culture usually allows.

His work helps explain why a civilization can accumulate facts, data, and technical power while still becoming starved for meaning, wisdom, and transformation.

That makes him essential for distinguishing between tools that merely improve propositional throughput and tools that support fuller human knowing.

## What he represents

Vervaeke represents a philosophical and cognitive framework centered on:
- relevance realization
- the meaning crisis
- wisdom as a cultivation of multiple forms of knowing
- the integration of cognition, practice, transformation, and participation

## Core contribution

For Grasp Tools, Vervaeke's most important contribution is the distinction between four kinds of knowing:
- propositional knowing
- procedural knowing
- perspectival knowing
- participatory knowing

This gives a much more precise way to ask what a tool is actually supporting.

A tool that excels only at propositional knowing may still leave the user impoverished in the very capacities that make life meaningful and intelligent.

## Why he matters for humane cognitive tools

Vervaeke helps define a stronger standard for humane design.
A humane cognitive tool should not be judged only by:
- correctness
- speed
- convenience
- information yield

It should also be judged by whether it supports:
- skill
- salience
- perspective shift
- participatory transformation

This is a crucial correction to tool cultures that optimize for answers while neglecting wisdom.

## Productive tension

Vervaeke does not primarily work as a designer or interface thinker.
He offers a deep account of human knowing, but not always concrete tool-design guidance.

This is where he productively meets Bret Victor and Andy Clark:
- Bret helps ask what kinds of media and representation recruit fuller cognition.
- Clark helps explain how tools and environments can become real parts of cognition.
- Vervaeke helps ask whether those tools support wisdom rather than only information processing.

## Relevance to this repo

A Grasp Tools lens informed by Vervaeke asks:

> Does this tool support only propositional efficiency, or does it cultivate the fuller ecology of human knowing required for meaning, wisdom, and transformation?
